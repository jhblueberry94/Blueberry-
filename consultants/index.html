The visibility problem consultants face

Most consultants post sporadically on LinkedIn, if at all. When they do, it tends to be reactive: sharing an article, congratulating a connection, occasionally writing something when a project wraps and the pipeline needs attention.

That pattern is almost exactly backwards. Research from the 6sense 2025 B2B Buyer Report found that B2B buyers complete 61% of their purchase decision before making first contact. The consultant already in a buyer's peripheral vision during that period is in a completely different commercial position to the one who sends a connection request the week the brief goes out.

The difficulty is that creating content consistently is genuinely hard when you are either billing your time or selling.

The consultants who solve this problem are not posting more often out of willpower. They have a system that removes the friction from the process.

Why LinkedIn is different when you work for yourself

An employee building their LinkedIn presence benefits from the firm's brand credibility behind them. As an independent, your credibility is entirely personal. There is no brand behind you.

That means generic content does almost nothing for your commercial position. What works is specific: the observation from a recent engagement, the counterintuitive finding from your field, the framing of a problem that makes a potential client feel understood before they have spoken to you.

That kind of content requires someone who can draw it out of you in a structured way and turn what comes out of a conversation into something you are genuinely comfortable putting your name to.

No one else has exactly your perspective. Done well, that is a genuine competitive advantage.

Common questions

Does LinkedIn actually generate work for independent consultants?

Yes, but the mechanism is different to what most consultants expect. Consistent content keeps you visible to your existing network, which accelerates referrals and reactivates relationships that might otherwise go quiet. According to the 6sense 2025 B2B Buyer Report, buyers complete 61% of their decision before making contact. Consultants visible on LinkedIn during that pre-contact phase are far more likely to be on the shortlist when the brief goes out.

How long does LinkedIn thought leadership take to produce results?

Realistically, six to twelve months to see consistent commercial impact. Most clients notice early signals within eight to ten weeks: more relevant profile views, more relevant connection requests, and the occasional DM from someone they would genuinely want to work with.

I am not a natural writer. Does that matter?

No. The Content Call model is built specifically for people who have a great deal to say but no desire to sit in front of a blank screen. You talk, we write. Your voice, your thinking, your name on it.

How often should a consultant post on LinkedIn?

Two to three times per week is the target for most consultants working with Blueberry Media. Only 3% of LinkedIn users post more than once per week, which means two to three posts per week puts you among a very small group of consistently visible voices in your field.

What should a consultant post on LinkedIn?

The content that performs is specific rather than generic. Observations from recent engagements (appropriately anonymised), counterintuitive findings from your field, frameworks that solve problems your clients commonly face, and honest commentary on sector trends from someone who has actually seen the inside of the organisations involved.
